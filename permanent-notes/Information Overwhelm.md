* Problems
    * 資訊爆炸/洪流的時代
        * In the age of
            * Information abundance
            * infinite information on the internet
    * "Never-Ending-Now" by David Perell
    * FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)
    * Most of us only consume contents created
        * within the last 24 hours (e.g., news)
        * recommended by the "For You" algorithm (e.g., YT videos, SM feeds)
* Solutions
    * Focus on JIT info, rather than JIC info
    * Be proactive rather than reactive
    * News Sobriety
    * The Lindy Library: 0.1% of resources/ideas that create 90% of the value
        * Only consume quality contents that have stood the test of time
    * Study history to predict the future
        * It's all just history repeating itself (Historic Recurrence).
        * Humans tend to repeat the mistakes humans make.
    * Digital Environment Design
        * Your digital environment shapes your thinking just as much (if not more) than your physical environment.
        * What you watch, what you read, who you listen to → These things should be pushing you towards your goals—not distracting you from them.
    * Low Information Diet-style media fast
        * Cull Your Social Media Feed
            * BS Asymmetry Principle
                * The energy required to refute bullshit is much larger than the energy required to produce it.
                * This is why BS spreads so easily—especially on social media.
    * Strategic Ignorance -> Ignorance is bliss
    * Franeworks/Methods for Information Organisation
        * LATCH (Location, Alphabet, Time, Category, Hierarchy) by Richard Saul Wurman (founder of TED)
        * Orthagonal Categories: categories are orthagonal when they are both mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive
* Remember
    * You're only as smart as the information you consume
    * What digital content we consume is as important as what we eat
    * 24 hours each day isn’t enough to consume 0.0001% of the world’s events.

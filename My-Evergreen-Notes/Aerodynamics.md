Drag: Understand different types of drag (form, skin friction, and induced) and how they affect a cyclist.

Streamlining: Learn techniques to reduce air resistance by optimizing the cyclist's position, clothing, and equipment.

Cross-Sectional Area: Recognize the impact of a cyclist's frontal area on aerodynamic efficiency and ways to minimize it.

Reynolds Number: Understand the concept of Reynolds number in fluid dynamics and its relevance to cycling aerodynamics.

Pressure Gradients: Explore how changes in air pressure around the cyclist influence aerodynamic forces.

Yaw Angle: Learn about the angle between the incoming air and the direction of motion (yaw angle) and its effect on aerodynamics.

Boundary Layer: Understand the boundary layer and its role in aerodynamic drag, especially in the context of bike surfaces.

Aerodynamic Forces: Comprehend the forces acting on a cyclist, including lift and side forces, and how they impact overall performance.

Wind Tunnel Testing: Familiarize yourself with wind tunnel testing methods for assessing aerodynamic efficiency in a controlled environment.

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD): Gain knowledge of CFD simulations to analyze and optimize aerodynamic designs digitally.

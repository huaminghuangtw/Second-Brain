Speak with integrity. Say only what you mean.
- People can't read your thoughts. If you think something, say something. Don't expect people to guess your thoughts and feelings. Don't hold a grudge when people didn't predict what you wanted them to do. Just tell them.

As [Neil Strauss](https://www.neilstrauss.com/) said, “*Unspoken expectations are premeditated resentments.*” I say, “*Shared expectations are adaptive gratification.*”

3 Steps to avoid "unspoken expectations":
- Start with facts and what you expect, specifically (think “painting done”[^painting_done])
- Describe why it’s an expectation or concern
- Ask a question to invite them into the dialogue

[^painting_done]: This means to fully walk through expectations of what the completed task will look like, including when it will be done, how it will be used, the context, the consequences of not doing it, the costs—everything you can think of to paint a shared picture of the expectations.
